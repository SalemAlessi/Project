Gas Turbine

What The Gas Turbine?

Gas Turbine is a combustion engine at the heart of a power plant that can convert natural gas or other liquid fuels to mechanical energy

What are the Basic Components

  • Compressor
    1. Draws in air & compresses it
  • Combustion Chamber
    1. Fuel pumped in and ignited to burn with compressed air
  • Turbine
    1. Hot gases converted to work
    2. Can drive compressor & external load

What are The Advantages and The Disadvantages ?

  • Advantages of Gas Turbine Power Plant
    1. They are small in size, weigh less and have low initial cost per unit output.
    2. They are easy to install within short periods.
    3. They are quick-starting and smooth running.
    4. They offer flexibility by supplying electricity for power generation as well as by supplying compressed air for process needs.
    5. They are capable of using a range of liquid and gaseous fuels including synthetic fuels.
    6. They are subjected (put) to fewer environmental restrictions than other prime movers.
    7. Water consumption is less compared to steam power plant.
  • Disadvantages of Gas Turbine Power Plant
    1. Gas turbine plants have less vibrations when compared with reciprocating engines of the same speed. However the high frequency noise from the compressor is objectionable.
    2. High temperatures impose severe restriction on the servicing conditions of the plant.
    3. Overall efficiency is low since two-thirds of the total power output is used for driving the compressor.
    4. The blades of the turbine require special cooling methods due to the severity of operating temperatures and pressures. In practice, the temperatures at the entry of the turbine are as high as 1100°C - 1260°C. Hence they should be made of special metals and alloys.
    5. They are incompatible with solid fuels.

What's The Compressor?

Supplies high pressure air for combustion process

  • Compressor Types
    1. Radial/centrifugal flow compressor
      1. Advantages: simple design, good for low compression ratios (5:1)
      2. Disadvantages: Difficult to stage, less efficient
    2. Axial flow compressor
      1. Good for high compression ratios (20:1)
      2. Most commonly used
    3. Controlling Load on Compressor
      1. To ensure maximum efficiency and allow for flexibility, compressor can be split into HP & LP sections
      2. Vane control: inlet vanes/nozzle angles can be varied to control air flow
    4. Compressor Stall
      1. Interruption of air flow due to turbulence
    5. Primary Air (30%)
      1. Passes directly to combustor for combustion process.
    6. Secondary Air (65%)
      1. Passes through holes in perforated inner shell & mixes with combustion gases.
    7. Film Cooling Air (5%)
      1. Insulates/cools turbine blades

What's The Combustuin Chamber?

  • Where air & fuel are mixed, ignited, and burned.
  • Spark plugs used to ignite fuel.
  • Types.
    1. Can: for small, centrifugal compressors.
    2. Annular: for larger, axial compressor.
    3. Can-annular: rarely used.

What's The Turbine?

  • Consists of one or more stages designed to develop rotational energy
  • Uses sets of nozzles & blades
  • Single shaft
    1. Power coupling on same shaft as turbine.
    2. Same shaft drives rotor of compressor and power components.
  • Split Shaft
    1. Gas generator turbine drives compressor.
    2. Power turbine separate from gas generator turbine.
    3. Power turbine driven by exhaust from gas generator turbine.
    4. Power turbine drives power coupling.

Gas Turbine Systems

  • Air System:
    1. Air intakes are located high up & multiple filters.
    2. Exhaust discharged out stacks.
  • Fuel System:
    1. Uses either DFM or JP-5.
  • Lubrication System:
    1. Supply bearings and gears with oil.

Gas Turbine Accessory Systems

  • Starting System:
    1. To get compressor initially rotated, HP air used (can use electrical also)
    2. Once at certain RPM, fuel injected and spark ignited
  • Power Transmission System:
    1. Reduction gears used to transfer torque.
    2. With split shaft, turbines can run @ different speeds.
