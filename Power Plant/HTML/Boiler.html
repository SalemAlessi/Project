What is the Boilers?

The equipment used for producing and transferring steam is called: Steam generators/Boilers.

  • Relative position of Hot gases and Water
    1. Fire Tube Boiler.

      2. The hot gases passes through the tubes that are surrounded by water. Fire tube boilers are also known by certain common names

      1. Horizontal Return Tubular
      2. Locomotive Fire Box
      3. Scotch Marine
      4. Vertical Tubular

    2. Water Tube Boiler

      3. The water passes through the tubes and the hot gases produced by combustion of fuel, flow outside. This type of Boilers designated by the following common names

      1. Babcock and Wilcox Boiler (straight but inclined tubes which connect the headers).
      2. Stirling Boiler (multitubular boiler having bent tubes that connect boiler drums to headers).
  • Method of firing
    1. Internally fired Boilers

      2. The furnace is provided inside the boiler shell and is completely surrounded by water cooled surfaces. This method of firing is used in

      1. Lancashire Boilers
      2. Locomotive Boilers
      3. Scotch Boilers

    2. Externally fired Boilers

      3. The furnace is provided outside/ under the boiler. It has an advantage that its furnace is simple to construct and can easily be enlarged, as and when required. This method of firing is used in Babcock and Wilcox Boiler.

  • Pressure of Steam
    1. High pressure Boilers

      2. Boilers producing steam 80 bar and above are called High pressure boilers. E.g.

      1. Babcock and Wilcox Boiler
      2. Lamont Boilers
      3. Velox Boilers
      4. Benson Boilers etc.
    1. Low pressure Boilers

      2. Boilers producing steam Lower than 80 bar are called Low pressure boilers. E.g.

      1. Cochran Boilers
      2. Cornish Boiler
      3. Lancashire Boiler
      4. Locomotive Boiler
  • Method of circulation of water
    1. Natural circulation method

      2. Circulation set up by convection current or by gravity.

    2. Forced circulation method

      3. Circulation set up for high pressure steam through pumps.

Manhole

This is provided at suitable position on the boiler shell so that the man can enter into boiler shell for inspection, maintenance and repairs. This hole is usually made in elliptical shape of the size convenient for a man to enter through this hole. The opening is closed by steam tight cover.

Steam Trap

Steam trap is used to collect and automatically drain away the water resulted from partial condensation of steam without steam to escape with this condensate through a valve. The valve after draining the condensate is closed. Is presses the leakage of steam from the trap

Antipriming Pipe

It is attached below the stop valve to avoid the water particles being carried away along with steam. It has closed pipe with closed ends of 1m and 2m length. The top side of the pipe has perforations. When the steam passes through it, due to inertia effect, the moisture of steam falls into the pipe and the steam with reduced moisture passes into the stop valve.
